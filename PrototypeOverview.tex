\section{High-Level System Description}

The prototype is an \textbf{automated, motor-driven ridge track with a dual-sided air nozzle carriage}. It verifies the core clearing mechanism of our Design Concept: a linear track that translates a high-velocity planar air blade along a roof ridge to dislodge wet debris without physical contact.

\begin{figure}[H]
\centering
\includegraphics[width=0.65\textwidth]{V2RidgeTrackSystem.jpeg}
\caption{As-built prototype: 60\,cm aluminum T-slot track with V2 pulleys, GT2 timing belt, Nema\,17 stepper motor, and nozzle carriage. D2FS-FL-N limit switches visible at each end. See also: \href{https://drive.google.com/drive/folders/1wNK_jYUvHXoMne_cIdYbAIURdvYpuG-J}{\textit{track overview video}}.}
\label{fig:asbuilt}
\end{figure}

\vspace{-4pt}
\begin{itemize}
    \item \textbf{Translation:} Nema\,17 stepper motor, A4988 driver, GT2 timing belt with custom 3D-printed pulleys, Raspberry Pi Pico (CircuitPython) with limit-switch-based reversal. 60\,cm track, 56\,cm active travel.
    \item \textbf{Clearing:} Dyson hair dryer (abstracting the final compressor; see \texttt{Air\_Source\_Abstraction\_Justification.pdf} in \texttt{04.4}) through a custom converging slit nozzle at 30$^\circ$, producing a planar shear layer. The carriage is dual-sided for bidirectional clearing.
\end{itemize}

\subsection{Power Segregation \& Thermal Tuning}
\label{sec:power}

\textbf{Power:} Shared rail caused Pico brownouts during motor draw. Fixed by isolating 5\,V USB (Pico logic, 3.3\,V to A4988 $V_{DD}$) from 12\,V DC adapter ($V_{MOT}$).
\textbf{Thermal:} A4988 $V_{REF}$ tuned to ${\sim}50\%$ to prevent thermal shutdown while holding torque.
\textbf{Firmware:} Polling-based state machine on GP15/GP16; instant DIR reversal on switch trigger; 4-cycle auto-stop after 2 round trips. Source: \href{https://github.com/memo-ozdincer/ESC203-AI-Prototypes/blob/master/dossier_artifacts/PraxisCode.py}{\texttt{PraxisCode.py}}.

\section{Prototype Design Process}

\subsection{Integration Changes}

Six changes bridged individual subsystems into a working system (\texttt{04.3\_Integration}; \href{https://drive.google.com/drive/folders/1wNK_jYUvHXoMne_cIdYbAIURdvYpuG-J}{\textit{integration videos}}):

\begin{enumerate}
    \item \textbf{Motor:} DC $\rightarrow$ Nema\,17 stepper (\S\ref{sec:motor}). \textit{Video:} \href{https://drive.google.com/drive/folders/1wNK_jYUvHXoMne_cIdYbAIURdvYpuG-J}{StruggleswithMovementTrack.mov}
    \item \textbf{Driver:} L298N H-Bridge $\rightarrow$ A4988 + screw terminals.
    \item \textbf{Power:} Single rail $\rightarrow$ isolated 5\,V / 12\,V (\S\ref{sec:power}).
    \item \textbf{Thermal:} $V_{REF}$ calibrated to 50\%.
    \item \textbf{Firmware:} \texttt{time.sleep()} $\rightarrow$ reactive polling state machine.
    \item \textbf{Deadzone:} Limit switches take ${\sim}$2\,cm/end; nozzle overhangs 2\,cm each side $\rightarrow$ 100\% coverage.
\end{enumerate}

\subsection{Verification}

Three-test protocol (Magnitude $\rightarrow$ Geometry $\rightarrow$ Wet Stiction) plus mechanical endurance. Full data and protocols in \texttt{04.4\_Verification}. All 8 nozzle trial videos: \href{https://drive.google.com/drive/folders/1RAJA95EF-XjMrkhjWkuoIBbONH_DbRVu}{\textit{[Google Drive]}}.

Force is conserved ($\sim$1.96\,N) across geometries within 12\,cm. Geometry redistributes force into planar shear rather than increasing it. Sharp drop at 16\,cm means the nozzle must stay within 12\,cm of the roof.

\subsubsection{Test 3: Wet Debris Stiction}
Saturated tissue on 40-grit sandpaper at $d = 50$\,cm (worst case; see \texttt{Debris\_Model\_Justification.pdf} in \texttt{04.4}):

\begin{itemize}
    \item \textbf{Circular:} \textcolor{red}{\textbf{FAILED.}} Pinned wet tissue flat. \href{https://drive.google.com/file/d/1wO-Wh8iFGygEvBvc3OOH91XQSwhoY_Ws/view}{\textit{[Video]}}
    \item \textbf{Air Blade:} \textcolor{green!50!black}{\textbf{PASSED.}} Shear layer peeled debris off surface. \href{https://drive.google.com/file/d/16htpF3ptwKEM5UxYE679x3f3xWmt80h1/view}{\textit{[Video]}}
\end{itemize}

\subsubsection{Endurance Test}
Continuous 2-minute cycle (exceeding 2-pass minimum). Zero stalling, belt held tension through reversals, A4988 within thermal limits. 56\,cm in 22\,s ($v \approx 2.55$\,cm/s). \href{https://drive.google.com/drive/folders/1wNK_jYUvHXoMne_cIdYbAIURdvYpuG-J}{\textit{[Full cycle video]}} \textit{(O-1.1-ELEC-1, O-1.3-SOFT-1, O-3.2-ELEC-1.)}

% 7  CHALLENGES
% ============================================================
\section{Challenges Faced}

\begin{enumerate}
    \item \textbf{Motor stalling:} DC + H-Bridge failed under load (\href{https://drive.google.com/drive/folders/1wNK_jYUvHXoMne_cIdYbAIURdvYpuG-J}{\textit{video}}). Required full 3-iteration drivetrain redesign.
    \item \textbf{Power brownouts:} Shared rail caused Pico resets. Rewired with isolated rails + screw-terminal blocks.
    \item \textbf{3D print tolerances:} FDM teardrop on axles (Fig.~\ref{fig:v1axle}). Sanded, then redesigned with thicker geometry.
    \item \textbf{Belt grip:} Smooth TPU slipped; hot glue failed on TPU. Redesigned belt with grip indents (Fig.~\ref{fig:v2belt}).
    \item \textbf{Mechanical deadzones:} Switches take ${\sim}$2\,cm/end. Nozzle overhang compensates.
    \item \textbf{A4988 thermal shutdown:} $V_{REF}$ tuned to 50\% to balance torque vs.\ heat.
\end{enumerate}

% 8  CONTRIBUTION
% ============================================================
\section{Contribution to Design Concept Understanding}

\textbf{(1) Automated translation works.} The stepper-driven belt system traverses reliably with mm-precision and survives 120\,s+ of continuous operation. A motor-driven ridge carriage is viable.

\textbf{(2) Planar air blade outperforms circular.} The verification chain (Force $\rightarrow$ Angle $\rightarrow$ Wet Stiction) proved the slit nozzle's shear-layer mechanism clears adhered wet debris where the circular nozzle fails.

\textbf{Next phase:} Stepper + isolated power (not DC); planar slit at ${\sim}30^\circ$ (Coanda); nozzle within 12\,cm of surface; compressor selection to replace Dyson abstraction.
