\section{High-Level Prototype System Description}

The prototype is an \textbf{automated, motor-driven ridge track with an attached dual-nozzle air carriage}, verifying the clearing mechanism of our Design Concept: a linear track translating a high-velocity planar air blade to autonomously dislodge wet debris without physical contact. It consists of two functional subsystems:

\begin{itemize}
    \item \textbf{Translation:} Nema~17 stepper motor, A4988 driver, GT2 timing belt \& custom pulleys, Raspberry Pi Pico (CircuitPython) with limit-switch-based automated reversal. Traverses a 60\,cm aluminum T-slot track.
    \item \textbf{Clearing:} Continuous high-volume air source (Dyson hair dryer, abstracting the final compressor) through a custom converging slit nozzle at 30$^\circ$ angle of attack, producing a high-velocity planar shear layer.
\end{itemize}

\section{Key Design Decisions}

\subsection{Motor \& Driver Evolution}
\label{sec:motor}

The drivetrain underwent three complete iterations, each driven by a specific failure mode discovered during testing.
Figure~\ref{fig:motor} shows the physical evolution; supporting artifacts (including a \href{https://drive.google.com/drive/folders/1wNK_jYUvHXoMne_cIdYbAIURdvYpuG-J}{video of the motor jitter failure}) are in \texttt{04.3\_Integration}.

\begin{figure}[H]
\centering
\begin{minipage}[t]{0.32\textwidth}
    \centering
    \includegraphics[width=\textwidth, height=2.6cm, keepaspectratio]{DC_Motor_Breadboard_Iteration1.jpeg}\\[1pt]
    {\scriptsize\textbf{Iter.\,1:} DC motor, breadboard, 9\,V.
    \textcolor{red}{Failed:} no dead reckoning.}
\end{minipage}\hfill
\begin{minipage}[t]{0.32\textwidth}
    \centering
    \includegraphics[width=\textwidth, height=2.6cm, keepaspectratio]{HBridge_Motor_Iteration2.jpeg}\\[1pt]
    {\scriptsize\textbf{Iter.\,2:} DC motor + L298N H-Bridge.
    \textcolor{red}{Failed:} voltage drop, stall, jitter.}
\end{minipage}\hfill
\begin{minipage}[t]{0.32\textwidth}
    \centering
    \includegraphics[width=\textwidth, height=2.6cm, keepaspectratio]{Stepper_A4988_ScrewTerminals_Final.jpeg}\\[1pt]
    {\scriptsize\textbf{Final:} Nema\,17 + A4988 + screw terminals.
    \textcolor{green!50!black}{Resolved:} mm-precise, no jitter.}
\end{minipage}
\vspace{2pt}
\caption{Electrical subsystem evolution (left $\rightarrow$ right). Each iteration addressed a critical failure of the previous.}
\label{fig:motor}
\end{figure}

\vspace{-6pt}
\begin{itemize}
    \item \textbf{DC $\rightarrow$ Stepper:} Time-based DC motor movement was unreliable due to variable friction and carriage mass. The Nema\,17 provides exact step-count positioning (0.2\,mm/step), enabling mm-perfect actuation.
    \item \textbf{H-Bridge $\rightarrow$ A4988:} The L298N suffered extreme voltage drops under the high-mass carriage, causing stalling and severe jitter. The A4988 provides micro-stepping torque control and eliminates jitter.
\end{itemize}

\subsection{Power Segregation \& Thermal Tuning}
\label{sec:power}

\textbf{Power Segregation:} Sharing a single rail caused logic brownouts during motor current draw. \textit{Solution:} Complete physical isolation---Pico on 5\,V USB (3.3\,V to A4988 $V_{DD}$), motor on dedicated 12\,V DC adapter to $V_{MOT}$.
\textbf{Thermal Tuning:} A4988 $V_{REF}$ potentiometer calibrated to ${\sim}50\%$, capping current to prevent thermal shutdown while maintaining holding torque.

\subsection{Firmware: Delays $\rightarrow$ Reactive State Machine}

CircuitPython logic uses reactive polling of limit switches (GP15/GP16, internal pull-up). When a switch is driven LOW, the DIR pin (GP2) reverses immediately. A cycle counter auto-stops the motor after two complete round-trips (4 switch events), and a dual-button reset allows restarting. Source: \texttt{PraxisCode.py} in \texttt{04.2\_Build/Software}.

\section{Prototype Design Process}

\subsection{Integration Changes}
\label{sec:integration}

Six key changes bridged individually-functional subsystems into a working integrated system (artifacts in \texttt{04.3\_Integration}):

\begin{enumerate}
    \item \textbf{Motor Substitution:} DC motor $\rightarrow$ Nema\,17 stepper for positional accuracy (\S\ref{sec:motor}).
    \item \textbf{Driver Architecture:} L298N H-Bridge $\rightarrow$ A4988 stepper driver + screw-terminal power blocks.
    \item \textbf{Power Segregation:} Single rail $\rightarrow$ isolated 5\,V logic / 12\,V motor (\S\ref{sec:power}).
    \item \textbf{Thermal Tuning:} $V_{REF}$ calibrated to 50\% to balance torque vs.\ thermal limits.
    \item \textbf{Firmware Upgrade:} Time-based delays $\rightarrow$ reactive limit-switch polling with 4-cycle auto-stop.
    \item \textbf{Deadzone Compensation:} Limit switches occupy ${\sim}$2\,cm per end; nozzle overhangs 2\,cm on each side of mount $\rightarrow$ 100\% surface coverage despite mechanical limits.
\end{enumerate}

\subsection{Verification}

Verification followed a structured three-test protocol for the fluid dynamics of the clearing mechanism, plus a mechanical endurance test.
All protocols and raw data are in \texttt{04.4\_Verification}.

\subsubsection{Test 1: Force Quantification (Magnitude)}
\label{sec:test1}

A Dyson hairdryer (max setting) was directed at a digital scale with a rigid 14$\times$14\,cm target.
Force was measured at four distances across three nozzle geometries.

\vspace{-2pt}
\begin{table}[H]
\centering\small
\caption{Continuous force output (N) vs.\ distance. Baseline $\approx 200$\,g ($\sim$1.96\,N).}
\label{tab:force}
\begin{tabular}{@{}lcccc@{}}
\toprule
\textbf{Nozzle Geometry} & \textbf{4\,cm} & \textbf{8\,cm} & \textbf{12\,cm} & \textbf{16\,cm} \\
\midrule
Pure Dyson (Baseline)    & 1.96 & 1.95 & 1.95 & 1.76 \\
Dyson OEM Air Blade      & 1.98 & 1.97 & 1.95 & 1.78 \\
Custom Air Blade (Ours)  & 1.96 & 1.95 & 1.93 & 1.72 \\
\bottomrule
\end{tabular}
\end{table}

\vspace{-4pt}
\textbf{Key Finding:} Total force is conserved ($\sim$1.96\,N) across all single-directional geometries within 12\,cm (mass-flow conservation). Geometry does not increase total force---it \textit{redistributes} it. This shifted design focus to optimizing \textit{planar shear stress}. Force degrades at 16\,cm, so the nozzle must remain within 12\,cm of the surface.

\subsubsection{Test 2: Distance \& Angle Optimization (Geometry)}
\label{sec:test2}

A 30$^\circ$ roof mock-up (35$\times$15\,cm, 40-grit aluminum oxide sandpaper simulating asphalt shingles) was tested at $d = 10$, 25, and 50\,cm.
Three trial sets per configuration; results were highly consistent.

\vspace{-2pt}
\begin{table}[H]
\centering\small
\caption{Clearing performance: Custom Air Blade vs.\ Circular Nozzle at 30$^\circ$ angle of attack.}
\label{tab:clearing}
\begin{tabularx}{\textwidth}{@{}l X X@{}}
\toprule
\textbf{Distance} & \textbf{Circular Nozzle} & \textbf{Custom Air Blade} \\
\midrule
10\,cm (Close)  & Full clear; geometry advantage negligible at close range & Full clear \\
25\,cm (Medium) & Cleared, debris blown lower \& shorter distance            & Debris ejected higher \& further; shear layer penetrates \textit{under} debris \\
50\,cm (Far)    & Narrow central path only; debris left on lateral edges (turbulent diffusion) & Wide uniform sweep across full 15\,cm width; coherent planar coverage \\
\bottomrule
\end{tabularx}
\end{table}

\vspace{-4pt}
The 30$^\circ$ angle balances the normal force $F_y$ (Coanda-effect boundary attachment, without pinning) against the tangential shear $F_x$ (propelling debris down-slope).
Steeper angles ($>$45$^\circ$) pin debris; shallower ($<$15$^\circ$) deflect over it. \textit{(Passes O-4.2-STRU-1.)}

\subsubsection{Test 3: Wet Debris Maximum Stiction Stress Test}
\label{sec:test3}

The critical test at $d = 50$\,cm with heavily saturated tissue paper on 40-grit sandpaper:

\vspace{-2pt}
\begin{itemize}
    \item \textbf{Circular Nozzle (30$^\circ$):} \textcolor{red}{\textbf{FAILED.}} Localized high-pressure column pinned wet tissues flat against the surface.
    \href{https://drive.google.com/file/d/1wO-Wh8iFGygEvBvc3OOH91XQSwhoY_Ws/view}{\textit{[Video: Circular Wet Test --- Pinned]}}
    \item \textbf{Custom Air Blade (30$^\circ$):} \textcolor{green!50!black}{\textbf{PASSED.}} High-velocity shear layer slipped beneath the water/sandpaper barrier, peeling saturated debris off the surface.
    \href{https://drive.google.com/file/d/16htpF3ptwKEM5UxYE679x3f3xWmt80h1/view}{\textit{[Video: Air Blade Wet Test --- Success]}}
\end{itemize}

\vspace{-2pt}
Water maximizes stiction and flattens aerodynamic profile. The circular nozzle's impact drag fails; the air blade's boundary-layer shear overcomes stiction. \textit{(Passes O-1.1-STRU-1, O-1.1-STRU-2.)} Full 8-trial set: \href{https://drive.google.com/drive/folders/1RAJA95EF-XjMrkhjWkuoIBbONH_DbRVu}{\textit{[All Trial Videos]}}.

\subsubsection{Endurance Testing \& Automated Reversal}

The system completed a \textbf{continuous 2-minute endurance cycle}---exceeding the 2-pass minimum. Results: \textbf{zero stalling}, belt tension maintained through reversals, A4988 within thermal limits. Active travel: 56\,cm in 22\,s ($v \approx 2.55$\,cm/s), providing sufficient dwell time. \textit{(Passes O-1.1-ELEC-1, O-1.3-SOFT-1, O-3.2-ELEC-1.)} \href{https://drive.google.com/drive/folders/1wNK_jYUvHXoMne_cIdYbAIURdvYpuG-J}{\textit{[Video]}}

\section{Challenges Faced}

\begin{enumerate}
    \item \textbf{Motor Stalling \& Jitter:} DC + H-Bridge failed under load $\rightarrow$ complete 3-iteration drivetrain redesign to stepper-based system.
    \item \textbf{Power Brownouts:} Shared rail caused Pico resets $\rightarrow$ full rewiring with isolated rails and screw-terminal blocks.
    \item \textbf{3D Print Tolerances:} FDM teardrop artifacts on axles $\rightarrow$ sanding, then redesign with thicker geometry and triangle brackets.
    \item \textbf{Belt Grip:} Smooth TPU slipped; hot glue failed $\rightarrow$ CAD-redesigned belt with molded grip indents.
    \item \textbf{Mechanical Deadzones:} Limit switches occupy ${\sim}$2\,cm/end $\rightarrow$ nozzle overhang compensates for 100\% coverage.
    \item \textbf{Thermal Shutdown:} A4988 overheated $\rightarrow$ $V_{REF}$ tuned to 50\% for torque/thermal balance.
\end{enumerate}

% 8  CONTRIBUTION TO DESIGN CONCEPT
% ============================================================
\section{Contribution to Design Concept Understanding}

The prototype verified two core hypotheses:
\textbf{(1) Automated linear translation is feasible.} The stepper-driven belt system reliably traverses the track with mm-precision and survives 120\,s+ continuous operation without stalling or belt slip, confirming a motor-driven ridge carriage as viable for autonomous clearing.
\textbf{(2) A planar air blade outperforms a circular nozzle.} The verification chain (Force $\rightarrow$ Angle $\rightarrow$ Wet Stiction) proved the custom slit nozzle's shear-layer mechanism is superior for dislodging adhered wet debris---the worst-case scenario.

\textbf{Next-phase implications:} Use a stepper-driven actuator (not DC) with isolated power rails; air delivery via planar slit at ${\sim}30^\circ$ (Coanda effect); nozzle within 12\,cm of roof surface (coherent flow envelope per Test~1).
