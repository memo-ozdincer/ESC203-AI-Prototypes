\section{Objective}
To rigorously verify the physical viability of the air blade clearing mechanism using accessible prototyping tools (Myhal fabrication or household items). This protocol replaces low-fidelity pulsed air sources (e.g., ball pumps) with continuous flow sources to accurately model the required fluid dynamics.

\section{Equipment Required}
\begin{itemize}
    \item \textbf{Continuous Air Source:} Hair dryer (set to cool) or shop vacuum exhaust.
    \item \textbf{Converging Nozzle:} Custom 3D printed nozzle or cardboard/duct-tape funnel tapering to a 2mm--5mm slit.
    \item \textbf{Test Surface:} Sloped cardboard or wood, surfaced with heavy-grit sandpaper or an actual asphalt shingle to accurately mimic roof friction.
    \item \textbf{Force Measurement:} Digital kitchen scale.
    \item \textbf{Test Debris:} Wet leaves (soaked and pressed onto the test surface).
    \item \textbf{Visualization (Optional):} Sewing thread/yarn (for tuft testing) or room humidifier/vaporizer.
\end{itemize}

\section{Testing Procedures}

\subsection{Test 1: Output Force Quantification}
\textbf{Purpose:} Prove the nozzle generates sufficient force to overcome the static friction (stiction) of wet leaves.
\begin{enumerate}
    \item Mount a flat, rigid target (e.g., cardboard) vertically onto the digital kitchen scale.
    \item Position the air nozzle exactly 10\,cm, 20\,cm, and 30\,cm away from the target.
    \item Turn on the air source and record the maximum continuous mass reading in grams.
    \item Convert grams to Newtons ($F = m \times 0.0098$) to establish the baseline continuous force.
\end{enumerate}

\subsection{Test 2: Angle of Attack Optimization}
\textbf{Purpose:} Determine the most efficient angle for shear flow generation and debris removal.
\begin{enumerate}
    \item Setup the textured sloped roof mock-up.
    \item Adhere wet leaves to the surface.
    \item Mount the air nozzle at fixed angles relative to the surface: $15^\circ$, $30^\circ$, and $45^\circ$.
    \item Measure and record the time taken to dislodge the leaves at each angle from a standardized distance.
\end{enumerate}

\subsection{Test 3: Fluid Flow Visualization (Coanda Effect)}
\textbf{Purpose:} Visually prove the air attaches to the roof surface (boundary layer attachment) rather than dispersing prematurely.
\begin{enumerate}
    \item Tape 1-inch strips of yarn/thread across the roof surface mock-up (Tuft Test).
    \item Blow the air blade across the surface; observe if the tufts lay flat against the slope.
    \item Alternatively, use a room humidifier to introduce mist into the intake or flow path to capture the shear layer on video.
\end{enumerate}

\section{Verification Matrix Integration}
The data collected from these tests must be directly linked back to the Engineering Specifications in the Design Dossier. For example, comparing the Newtons of force measured in Test 1 to the calculated minimum force required to break wet leaf stiction.
